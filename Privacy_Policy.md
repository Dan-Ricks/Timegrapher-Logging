## Overview

Timegrapher Logging is a free, ad-free utility app designed for watchmakers and horologists to log and analyze timegrapher readings. We respect your privacy and are committed to being transparent about how the app handles your information.

## Information We Do Not Collect

Timegrapher Logging does **not** collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information, usage data, or analytics.

- No user accounts or sign-in required
- No data sent to any servers
- No analytics, tracking, or telemetry
- No advertising or third-party SDKs
- No collection of device identifiers, location, or contacts

All data you enter (timepiece name, rates, amplitudes, beat errors, etc.) remains **entirely on your device**.

## Permissions and Data Usage

The app requests access to your device's **Photo Library** for one purpose only:

- When you tap **"Save Timegrapher results to camera roll"**, the app generates an image from the data *you have entered* and saves it to your Photos app.
- This is a user-initiated action.
- The saved images are standard photos on your device. The app does not read, modify, or upload any existing photos.

The exact permission message shown by iOS is:
> "This app needs access to save screenshots of your timegrapher readings and poising data to your photo library."

You can revoke this permission at any time in iOS Settings > Timegrapher Logging.

## Optional Tips

Optional tips can be purchased via in-app purchase. No personal information is collected or stored for tips.

See Apple's privacy policy for in-app purchases.

## Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

## Changes to This Policy

## How to Get a Public URL (No Website Required)

### Easiest: GitHub Gist (30 seconds)
1. Go to https://gist.github.com and sign in with GitHub.
2. Create a new gist.
3. Title it `Privacy_Policy.md`.
4. Paste the entire content of this file (or use `Privacy_Policy.html`).
5. Set it to **Public**.
6. Click **Create secret gist** or **Create public gist**.
7. Click the **Raw** button at the top.
8. Copy that long raw URL and paste it into App Store Connect.

Example raw URL format:  
`https://gist.githubusercontent.com/yourusername/xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx/raw/Privacy_Policy.md`

### Nicer URL: Netlify Drop (free, instant)
1. Open https://app.netlify.com/drop
2. Drag and drop the file `Privacy_Policy.html` (the single-file HTML version in this folder) onto the page.
3. Netlify will instantly give you a URL like `https://amazing-unicorn-12345.netlify.app`
4. Use that URL in App Store Connect.

This gives you a clean, professional-looking page with almost zero effort.

### More Permanent: GitHub Pages
1. Create a new public repository on GitHub (you can name it `privacy` or anything).
2. Upload `Privacy_Policy.html` (rename it to `index.html` if you want the root URL).
3. Go to the repo → Settings → Pages.
4. Set "Source" to your main branch and save.
5. Your policy will be available at `https://yourusername.github.io/reponame/`

**Recommended for most people:** Use the **Netlify Drop** method with the HTML file. It looks the most professional.
